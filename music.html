Ecommerce Industry

With over 150 million buyers in India, the E-commerce sector in present days is witnessing an unprecedented rise. The potential for e-commerce is infinite where people of different age groups shop online while sitting comfortably at home.

There are two types of transactions in E-commerce Sector:

B2B - Under this type, the e-commerce transaction is conducted between the two distinct businesses
B2C - Under this type, e-commerce transaction includes the sale of goods, services from a business to public in general i.e. ultimate consumer

For smooth functioning, it is essential that the appropriate laws be made so that any kind of fraud may be prevented and the interest of the consumers who shop online be protected

The Indian Contract Act, 1872 and Information Technology Act, 2008

Under The Indian Contract Act, 1872, e-contracts are formed where parties which are similar to any other agreement online. The rule dictates:
a) There must be a lawful consideration for the contract
b) The parties must be competent to enter into a contract

Under the Information Act: The provisions are contained under the Information Technology Act, 2008. Section 10 A of the IT Act, 2008 provides for the validity of the contract entered through electronic modes. It says whenever the communication and acceptance of the proposal, its revocation and acceptances are made in electronic form, such contract shall not be deemed to be invalid or unenforceable solely on the ground that electronic means are used for the purpose.

Under the Section 66 A of the IT Act, 2008 a penalty is imposed wherein whosoever person dishonestly uses the password, identity of other shall be punished with imprisonment that may extend to 2 years or with fine of 1,00,000 rupees or both.

In addition to this, Section 84 A of the Act cast a duty on the Central Govt. for the promotion of e-governance and e-commerce. It also has to provide for secure use of electronic means.

Provisions regarding the data protection are contained in Section 43 A of the IT Act, 2008.
