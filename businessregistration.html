Business Registration in India

Registration

Before you starting any business you need to wisely choose the type of entity for your industry i.e. whether you want to register your business as a company, sole proprietor, partnership or any other form. To register a business under entertainment and media you can choose to register it as company, a limited partnership, or a limited liability company, one person company, and partnership firm. There are number of reasons for choosing one form of entity over another due to its advantage over another. There are few things to be kept in mind while choosing any form of entity i.e. cost involved, target market, how much profit a business will be able to make etc.

Registering your business entity as company is highly recommended due to its various features like a company can act as an artificial person, it has perpetual existence which is not affected by the death, insolvency or lunacy of its members, the feature of limited liability or the ability of company to sue, separation of ownership from management and many more features make it viable form of business entity.

People who have the same idea of achieving some common goal can work together by forming an association or company. Partnership and Company are two of the common ways of achieving a common commercial objective instead of operating as a sole proprietor. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is one such mechanism to work towards the attainment of commercial objective. The liability of members in LLP is limited to their agreed contribution in the LLP. Therefore it can be said to be a blend of both partnership as well as corporation. LLP is a separate legal entity which is capable of entering into contracts and holding property in its name. No partner can be held liable for the unauthorised action of the other partner. Hence, we can say in layman language that Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is the pro version of partnership.

Unlike other forms of entity One Person Company (OPC) requires only person as its member as the name suggests. It is similar to a Private Ltd Company except it requires only one person whereas in private company at least two members are required. Entrepreneurs in the initial stage of business prefer such form of entity due to various privileges such provision relating to meeting and quorum do not apply to them, and less formalities as compared to other form of business entity.

Partnership firm is preferred over companies due to its minimal compliance as compared to the company. Partnership is governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 which regulates all the partnership firms and their activities. Each member of the partnership firm is called partner. The minimum number of members a partnership firm needs to have is 2 and maximum number of partners a partnership firm could have is 10 in case of banking and 20 in case other businesses.

Although Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is a form of partnership, however it is different from the conventional form of Partnership Firm. Here are few of the differences:

LLP vsPartnership Firm 1. Registration of LLP is mandatory. Registration is not mandatory, it can be either registered or unregistered.

2. The liability of members is limited to their contribution in the firm. The liability is not limited and can extend to their personal asset.

3. One partner is not held liable for the action of another partner. Actions of one partner can hold other partners also liable
