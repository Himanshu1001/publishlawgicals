Question

What is the impact of GST implementation?

GST has simplified indirect text and Jurisdictional laws. However, for e-commerce players, GST Law has increased the Compliance requirement with a separate chapter on electronic commerce with special emphasis on tax collection at source (TCS) provisions.
TCS provisions were suspended till 30 June 2018.
The GST law as also introduce the definition of Electronic Commerce, Electronic Commerce operator and also mandated compulsory registration for E-Commerce operator, which implies that the focus of the government is now shifting to the E-Commerce industry, with the introduction of GST, the focus will shift more on commercial viability instead of tax saving for analysing the need for setting up facilitation centres/warehouses in various state by The E-Commerce operator/seller.

Feedback:- During the term, each party shall provide the other party with feedback regarding the other party’s product and services. “Feedback” means any requirement ,expectation, suggestion, test result, error data, bug report or other information and materials provided by one party to other party regarding such other party’s products or services.The party providing search feedback irrevocably assigns to the other party all of the providing party right, title and interest in and to the feedback including all the patents rights, copyright right, trade secret rights, and other intellectual property rights.
Residuals :- Each party has the right to use for any purpose the residuals resulting for access to or work with the other party’s confidential information. The term “Residuals'' means information in non-tangible form that is retained in the memories of persons who have had access to other parties confidential information, including any generalized ideas, concepts, known how or techniques contained in it.Nothing in this section or otherwise will be deemed to grant to either party a licence under any of other party's patents or copyright. Neither party shall have any obligations to limit or restrict the agreement of such person to pay royalties for any work resulting from the use of residuals.

Rules as to communication and acceptance of proposals, revocation,and contract formation between customers, sellers and the Marketplace provider. 2. Information Technology act 2000 and General data protection regulation (GDPR)
Companies under it (reasonable security practices and procedures are sensitive personal data or information) rules, 2011.
Intermediary Rules, 2011 under the IT Act stipulates the regulation relating to the content displayed on the intermediary website especially pertaining to defamation and obscenity.
U/S- 79 of IT Act Certain safe harbours are available to E- commerce entities functioning as “Intermediaries”.
Regulation applicable to “Intermediaries” relating to the content displayed on the portal especially pertaining to defamation and obscenity.
If the end consumer happens to be an EU resident, GDPR compliance becomes mandatory.
Issues related to data protection standard and data security.If the end consumer happen to be EU residents,GDPR compliance may also ensure.

3. Intellectual Property Issues
The Entity must secure all the trademarks and copyrights intended to be used by it, one must also be mindful to not infringe the trademark and copyright of other business as well.
Selling counterfeit goods and misuse of trademark rights by sellers listed on platforms is a significant challenge and must be dealt with by the platform operator to avoid prosecution.
In the age of such wide use of internet E- commerce entities shall be aware of various intellectual property infringement
that may happen online such as cybersquatting, identity theft, Copyright infringement, caching, derivative works,domain name protection and etc.
These are some added steps that ecom entities have to take, as per the Draft policy.

4. Payment and settlement systems Act, 2007 and other RBI regulation on payment mechanism.
Under the law “payment system” means a system that enables payment to be effected between a payer and a beneficiary, involving clearing.payment of settlement services all of them, but does not include a stock exchange. An E- commerce entity has to make sure if it qualifies as a payment system and shall comply accordingly.
As per the RBI notification dated 24 November 2009,it is mandatory for an intermediary which is receiving payment through electronic Modes to have a nodal account in operation for settling the payment of the merchants on its online e-commerce platform.
Further depending on envisaged arrangements for payments for the transactions on the portal,the entity must comply with the relevant rules relating to online payments made by the RBI.

5. Labelling and Packaging
An e-commerce entity as the product listed on its platform must conform to labelling and packaging norms set by the regulations made under relevant laws and the rules therein such as :-
Legal metrology Act, 2009
Food safety and standard Act, 2006.
Drugs and cosmetics Act,1940 etc.

6. Legal Metrology Act,2009 r/w legal metrology (packaged commodity rules,2011).
The web platform must display requisite information about the goods displayed on sale, such as units, dimensions, weights etc.on product page itself.

7. Sales,Shipping refunds and returns.
The entity must have in place an adequate policy dealing with sales and shipping relating to the legal incidence of transfer of property in goods, and other aspects of sales such as warranties and conditions etc.are covered under sales of goods act Act, 1930.
The entity must also have in place, in clear words,a return and refund policy to be adhered by the sellers and buyers.

8. Consumers Protection / Dispute resolution
As a provider of goods or services under the consumer protection Act, 1986 the entity must have in place adequate policies to address consumers complaints. Moreover, it is advisable for the ecommerce platforms to have meditations and arbitration mechanisms in place as well.

9. Competition Issues
Fixation of prices by arrangements between sellers listed on the platform and the entity,exclusive sales agreement and other practices and the scope of section 3 and 4 and the competition Act, 2002 can be brought under the scrutiny of the competition Commission of India. The entity must be mindful of these factors which enter into any agreement which May leverage in exciting dominance in the market, or work towards the creation of foreclosure or entry barriers in the relevant market.

10. GST Applicability
Irrespective of whether the annual turnover of the entity is lower than prescribed threshold,E commerce operators are not Eligible for composition levy scheme under GST laws of India. Moreover, it is mandatory for all e-commerce operators and sellers/ distributors/ suppliers who sell through E-Commerce to get GST registration in all states where they purport to sell their goods/ services.

11. Other local and laws and sector specific laws
The premises from which the business is run, and the manufacturing, warehousing and other aspects of the business will be continued to governed by factor specify law and local laws applicable. Due adherence to such laws must also be ensued.
